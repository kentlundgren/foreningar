About the review

An audit is an independent check of the association's finances – a bit like having an outsider double-check that the cash book adds up. We verify that money has been recorded correctly, that the bank balance matches the accounts, and that there are no unexplained discrepancies. The goal is confidence: that the board and the association's members can trust the figures.

The audit has two parts: accounts review (checking that the bookkeeping is correct) and governance review (checking that the board made its decisions in line with the association's statutes).

As of 2026-04-08 the balance report showed +SEK 1,316,633. However, entry Y2 (account 8999, SEK 881,658) has been identified as incorrect – see section below. Once Y2 is reversedStorno / reversal – accounting term

From Italian stornare ("to turn back"). Used in bookkeeping since the 19th century, via German stornieren and French storne.

In Visma, Fortnox and Bokio there is an explicit function called "Storno" or "Reverse" – it cancels an erroneous journal entry by creating a mirror-image counter-entry with swapped debit/credit.

Storno and reversal are synonyms; storno is the classical accounting term., the correct calculated result is +SEK 434,975.
