About the review

An audit is an independent check of the association's finances – a bit like having an outsider double-check that the cash book adds up. We verify that money has been recorded correctly, that the bank balance matches the accounts, and that there are no unexplained discrepancies. The goal is confidence: that the board and the association's members can trust the figures.

The audit has two parts: accounts review (checking that the bookkeeping is correct) and governance review (checking that the board made its decisions in line with the association's statutes).

Review notes as of 2026-04-09

Daniel's response – entry Y2 explained and reversal requested

Daniel Kirk responded on 2026-04-09 explaining the reasoning behind entry Y2 (debit 2099 / credit 8999, SEK 881,657). He believed it was necessary to manually book the year's result as a verifikation in order to "capture" it in the accounts. Kent sent a detailed reply the same day explaining why this is incorrect and requesting that Y2 be reversed.

Why Y2 is incorrect: The accounting system (Visma) calculates the year's result automatically – no manual entry is needed. Crediting account 8999 (an income account) adds SEK 881,657 as new revenue, which inflates the calculated result by that same amount. The entry is circular: the result includes its own booking.

Correct result once Y2 is reversed: SEK 434,975 (organic result SEK 419,046 + inventory changes SEK 15,929).

When the disposition is booked: After the annual meeting approves the appropriation, the following entry is made in the 2026 books: debit 2099 "Årets resultat" SEK 434,975 / credit 2010 "Balanserat resultat" SEK 434,975. Y2 reversal awaited
